Working Process

Reverse Osmosis forces raw feed water through semi-permeable membranes under hydraulic pressure. This isolates pure water molecules from dissolved solutes, chemical deposits, and organic particulates.

  1. Pre-treatment: Sand media and carbon filters remove suspended solids and neutralize chlorine vectors to protect RO membranes.
  2. Pressurization: Multistage centrifugal pumps elevate pressure above natural osmotic thresholds.
  3. Membrane Separation: Solute molecules are rejected at the membrane face, forming a concentrated waste stream (brine) and a pure permeate stream.
  4. Post-treatment: Permeate is subjected to pH correction, UV sterilization, and optional mineral replenishment.

Design Benefits

  • Highly resistant composite membrane layers
  • Energy recovery devices to recycle hydraulic pressure
  • Integrated clean-in-place (CIP) membrane scrub systems
